Top Places to Visit in Dehradun

Explore the Best Tourist Attractions

1

Forest Research Institute

The Forest Research Institute is a magnificent colonial structure spread across 450 hectares. Established in 1906, this British-era building features stunning Greco-Roman architecture and houses six museums dedicated to forestry. The institute is surrounded by well-manicured lawns and is a popular spot for morning walks, photoshoots, and educational tours. The museums showcase various aspects of forestry, including pathology, social forestry, and timber.

2

Robber's Cave (Guchhupani)

Located 8 km from the city center, Robber's Cave is a unique 600-meter-long cave formation where a stream disappears and reappears at different points. This natural wonder features cold water springs and small waterfalls, making it a popular spot for adventure enthusiasts. The cave got its name from the British era when it was allegedly used by robbers as a hideout. Today, it's a perfect destination for nature lovers and photographers.

Read More
3

Sahastradhara

Known as 'Thousand Fold Spring', Sahastradhara is famous for its sulphur water springs with medicinal properties. The water drips from limestone stalactites, creating a spectacular cave-like formation. Located 14 km from Dehradun, this spot offers cable car rides, cave exploration, and therapeutic bathing. The surrounding mountains and valleys make it a photographer's paradise.

Read More
4

Mindrolling Monastery

Home to the world's largest Buddhist stupa, this monastery showcases stunning Tibetan architecture. The 185-feet tall stupa contains relics, murals, and artifacts depicting Buddha's life. The monastery includes a college, meditation center, and beautiful gardens. Visitors can explore traditional Tibetan art, attend meditation sessions, and learn about Buddhist culture.

Read More
5

Tapkeshwar Temple

This ancient cave temple dedicated to Lord Shiva features naturally dripping water on the Shivling. The temple complex includes several small caves, meditation spots, and a natural sulfur spring. During Shravan month, thousands of devotees visit to offer holy water. The peaceful surroundings and spiritual atmosphere make it a must-visit destination.

Read More
6

Malsi Deer Park

Located at the foothills of the Shivalik range, this mini zoological park is home to various species of deer, birds, and rabbits. The park features beautiful landscaped gardens, children's play area, and walking trails. Perfect for family outings, it offers a chance to observe wildlife in a natural setting while enjoying mountain views.

Read More
7

Lacchiwala

A refreshing retreat with crystal clear water pools surrounded by Sal forests. This eco-park offers swimming, nature walks, and picnic spots. The man-made pools with clean spring water are perfect for a summer dip. The site includes changing rooms, food stalls, and well-maintained gardens making it ideal for day outings.

Read More
8

Clock Tower (Ghanta Ghar)

The iconic landmark of Dehradun, this six-faced clock tower stands at the heart of the city. Built in 1953, it's surrounded by bustling markets and food joints. The colonial architecture and central location make it a popular meeting point. The evening atmosphere with lit-up tower and vibrant market life is particularly enchanting.

Read More
9

Buddha Temple

A serene Japanese-style Buddhist temple featuring traditional architecture and peaceful gardens. The temple complex includes meditation halls, prayer wheels, and beautiful statues. Regular meditation sessions and Buddhist teachings are conducted here. The tranquil atmosphere and architectural beauty make it a must-visit spiritual destination.

Read More
10

Rajaji National Park

Spanning over 820 sq km, this national park is renowned for its elephant population and diverse wildlife. Home to tigers, leopards, deer, and over 315 bird species. Visitors can enjoy jeep safaris, nature walks, and bird watching. The park offers both wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers a chance to experience the rich biodiversity of the region.

Read More
11

Paltan Bazaar

The heart of Dehradun's shopping scene, Paltan Bazaar is famous for traditional handicrafts, woolens, and local delicacies. This vibrant market features shops selling Basmati rice, jewelry, and traditional Garhwali clothing. The evening buzz with street food stalls and local vendors creates an authentic shopping experience.

Read More
12

Shikhar Fall

A hidden gem nestled in the Mussoorie range, Shikhar Fall offers a pristine waterfall experience. The trek through dense forest leads to this spectacular cascade. Perfect for nature photography and adventure seekers, the fall provides a refreshing escape from city life with its crystal-clear pools and peaceful surroundings.

Read More
13

Khalanga War Memorial

A historic monument commemorating the Anglo-Gurkha War of 1814. This unique memorial honors both the British and Gurkha soldiers. The site offers panoramic views of the Doon Valley and houses important historical artifacts. The peaceful surroundings and historical significance make it a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Read More
14

Chandrabani Temple

An ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Chandrabani, located near the Rispana River. The temple complex includes sacred pools and meditation spots. During Navratri, it becomes a major pilgrimage site. The peaceful atmosphere and architectural beauty attract both devotees and tourists throughout the year.

Read More
15

Dehradun Zoo

A well-maintained zoological park housing various species of mammals, birds, and reptiles. The zoo focuses on wildlife conservation and education. Features spacious enclosures, educational programs, and guided tours. Perfect for family visits, it offers a chance to learn about wildlife in a natural setting.

Read More
16

Dehradun Botanical Garden

A serene botanical garden featuring a wide range of plant species from different parts of the world. The garden is divided into various sections, each showcasing unique plant collections. It offers a peaceful retreat for nature lovers, with picturesque landscapes and well-maintained lawns. Perfect for a peaceful stroll or a picnic with family and friends. The garden also hosts various events and workshops on plant conservation and horticulture.

Read More
17

Guru Ram Rai Darbar

A magnificent 17th-century temple complex featuring stunning murals and paintings. The architecture blends Mughal and Rajasthani styles. The walls showcase intricate artwork depicting religious scenes, historical events, and folk tales. The annual fair here attracts thousands of devotees.

Read More
18

Santala Devi Temple

Perched atop a hill, this temple offers panoramic views of Dehradun valley. The trek to the temple is adventurous and scenic. Known for its spiritual significance and peaceful environment, it's especially beautiful during sunrise and sunset.
