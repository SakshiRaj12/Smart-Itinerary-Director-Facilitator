About Dehradun

Gateway to the Himalayas: Where Heritage Meets Modern Aspirations

🏛️

Rich Heritage

Founded in 1676 by Guru Ram Rai, Dehradun has evolved from a spiritual sanctuary to Uttarakhand's dynamic capital. The city's name reflects its rich history - 'Dehra' (camp) of Guru Ram Rai and 'Dun' (valley), nestled in the Doon Valley.

  • Ancient Buddhist temples and monasteries
  • British-era architecture
  • Historical freedom movement sites
🎓

Knowledge Capital

Renowned as India's premier educational hub, hosting institutions that shape the nation's future leaders.

  • Forest Research Institute (Est. 1906)
  • Indian Military Academy
  • Wildlife Institute of India
  • Doon School (India's Eton)
  • ONGC Training Institute
🌿

Natural Paradise

A perfect blend of Himalayan majesty and valley serenity at 435m elevation.

🌡️ Climate: 15°C - 30°C 🌧️ Rainfall: 2073mm/year
  • Surrounded by Shivalik Hills
  • Rivers: Song, Tons & Rispana
  • Rich biodiversity
  • Tea gardens & Basmati fields
🎨

Cultural Mosaic

Experience the vibrant blend of Garhwali and Kumaoni traditions.

Festival Calendar

  • Basant Panchami (Spring Festival)
  • Bissu Fair (Harvest Celebration)
  • Jhanda Fair (March)

Local Delicacies

Bal Mithai • Kandali Saag • Pahadi Raita

Travel Essentials

Everything You Need for Your Dehradun Adventure

🌤️

Best Time to Visit

Peak Season

March to June

Temperature: 15°C - 30°C

Off Season

July to September

Monsoon period with occasional rainfall
🚆

How to Reach

✈️ By Air

Jolly Grant Airport (25 km)

Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai

🚂 By Train

Dehradun Railway Station

Well connected to major cities

🚌 By Road

Regular buses from Delhi, Chandigarh

6-7 hours from Delhi
🎒

What to Pack

  • Light woolens (October-February)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Rain gear (July-September)
  • Sun protection
  • Basic medications
  • Camera gear
💡

Travel Tips

  • Book accommodations in advance during peak season
  • Local transport: Auto-rickshaws, taxis readily available
  • Carry cash as some places might not accept cards
  • Try local cuisine at famous eateries
  • Visit FRI on weekdays to avoid crowds
